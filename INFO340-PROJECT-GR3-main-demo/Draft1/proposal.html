The UW campus building is dispersed, and the restroom environment is a long-standing issue that has plagued all faculties and students. While the map provides basic information about the location of the restrooms, it cannot do the job of displaying other information such as sanitary conditions, busyness, and quality of the facilities. ðŸ“± App Description

Dubloo is a university community-centered web app that helps members of the UW community find the best restrooms on campus, as rated by real users. Users of our app are students, faculty, staff, and visitors to the UW campus, especially those in need of accessible, private, or inclusive restrooms.Dubloo enhances the user experience of using restrooms on the UW campus by increasing the transparency and accuracy of restroom information.

Users can view restroom data taken from a map of public UW facilities, which is combined with real-time user ratings and messages. This hybrid approach ensures immediate availability of essential data, while user comments enrich the platform over time.

Users will interact with the app through:

  • Review submission forms to leave star ratings, comments, and upload photos of restrooms
  • Advanced filtering (e.g., "wheelchair accessible", "gender neutral", "single occupancy") to personalize search results
  • Interactive restroom map that updates with community reviews and highlights top-rated or frequently visited restrooms

By transforming toilet visits into community-contributed datasets, Dubloo provides a scalable solution to everyday problems. The web app not only saves time and improves convenience, but also allows users to contribute to the improvement of facilities they value. In short, Dubloo makes campus life cleaner, easier to navigate, and more respectful.
